Frequently Asked Questions

Generative AI relies on sophisticated machine learning models called deep learning models algorithms that simulate the learning and decision-making processes of the human brain. These models work by identifying and encoding the patterns and relationships in huge amounts of data, and then using that information to understand users' natural language requests or questions and respond with relevant new content.

There are three phases for Generative AI to operate. The first one is training, to create a foundation model that can serve as the basis of multiple gen AI applications. The second one is tuning, to tailor the foundation model to a specific gen AI application. Lastly, Generation, evaluation and retuning, to assess the gen AI application's output and continually improve its quality and accuracy.

There are many GenAI tools that are used today, ChatGPT is the most widely used, ChatGPT is an AI chatbot that can have human-like conversations, answer questions, and generate various types of content such as articles, code, emails, and creative text.

GenAI can produce what seems like diverse and original outputs. It can create content through capturing nuances in language, based on patterns which we may not have seen before in the data they were trained on. This helps to open up different perspectives and can give us ideas on how to explore topics from a variety of viewpoints.

The limitations or risk that GenAI have is like as with other AI models, generative AI models are trained on data sets and when this data contains bias it is reflected in the output of the model. Examples have been seen with text to image generators, which generate outputs that amplify gender and racial stereotypes or chatbots that demonstrate political bias.
Other FAQs

GenAI detection software is currently unreliable and known to produce false positives, so open conversation and clear guidelines are recommended over detection tools.

When using third-party GenAI solutions, sensitive data might be exposed as user requests often travel over the public internet. Best practices include using secure enterprise solutions that do not use customer data for model training.

The user or author is ultimately responsible for the accuracy, integrity, and originality of the content produced using GenAI tools. Content should always be reviewed and validated by a human.
